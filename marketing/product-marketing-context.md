## Mission

We rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome cats that have been abandoned, injured, or born on the streets of Poland. We believe every cat deserves a safe, loving home — not just the young, healthy, and beautiful ones. We especially fight for senior cats, sick cats, and cats with trauma histories who are routinely overlooked in shelters.

We run:
- A cat adoption programme (główny cel strony)
- Foster home network (domy tymczasowe)
- TNR (trap-neuter-return) for feral colonies
- Voluntary post-adoption support

## Audience

**Primary:** Polish adults aged 22–45, living in cities (Warszawa, Kraków, Wrocław, Poznań, Gdańsk), in apartments. Often first-time adopters. They want a cat but feel uncertain about the process. They are emotionally motivated — they saw the cat on Instagram, they want to help, they feel guilty for "buying instead of adopting."

**Secondary:** Families with children looking for a calm, gentle cat. Remote workers who want company. People who recently lost a pet and are ready to adopt again.

**Tertiary:** Donors and volunteers — animal lovers who cannot adopt but want to contribute financially or with time.

**What they fear:**
- The adoption process being complicated or judgmental
- Getting rejected
- Choosing the wrong cat for their lifestyle
- Hidden costs
- The cat being sick or traumatised without knowing

**What they want to feel:**
- Welcomed, not interrogated
- That they are doing something genuinely good
- That the cat's personality has been honestly described
- That they will be supported after adoption

## Tone of Voice

**Warm. Honest. Human. Never guilt-tripping.**

Inspired by the best Polish charities (Koty z Grochowa, Koci Zakątek Wrocław) and international benchmarks (Oregon Humane Society, Tierheim Berlin).

- Speak directly to the reader: "Ty" form, personal and warm
- Never guilt-trip or dramatise suffering to manipulate
- Be honest about cat personalities — shy cats are described as shy, not "just need love"
- Use humour sparingly but naturally — Poles appreciate wit
- Avoid corporate NGO-speak ("realizujemy misję", "działamy na rzecz")
- Avoid overselling: no "perfect cat for everyone", cats have flaws and that's ok

**Examples of good tone:**
- "Mochi szuka kogoś, kto rozumie, że przyjaźń wymaga czasu."
- "Nie każdy kot lubi być brany na ręce. Filek też nie. Za to mrucząc obok ciebie przez całą noc, da ci to samo."
- "Adopcja to nie zakup. To decyzja."

**Examples of bad tone to avoid:**
- "Ten biedny kot desperacko potrzebuje domu!" ← manipulative
- "Daj mu szansę na miłość!" ← cliché
- "Razem możemy zmienić świat!" ← NGO-speak

## Unique Positioning

1. **Honest cat profiles** — we describe cats as they actually are, including quirks, fears, and needs. This builds trust and reduces returns.
2. **Post-adoption support** — we don't disappear after signing the contract. Volunteers are available for advice.
3. **No judgment process** — we ask questions to find the right match, not to gatekeep.
4. **Foster network** — cats live in real homes, not cages. Adopters know exactly what they're getting.

## Key Polish Market Context

- Over 110,000 animals brought to shelters in Poland in 2023 alone
- Polish law (Ustawa o ochronie zwierząt 1997) gives free-roaming cats legal right to their habitat
- 1.5% podatek — donors can redirect 1.5% of their income tax to registered charities (KRS) at zero cost to themselves — **this is a huge, underused CTA**
- City programmes: many Polish cities offer up to 500 PLN subsidy for spaying/neutering/microchipping — worth mentioning
- Polish adopters respond strongly to: personal stories, before/after, real names, real photos
- Polish adopters are sceptical of: overly polished corporate charity aesthetics, excessive English
