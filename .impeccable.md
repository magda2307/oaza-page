## Design Context

### Users

**Primary — hesitant adopters.** People who *want* a cat but have been told no by other shelters, or who worry about taking on a cat with illness, age, or trauma. They arrive uncertain, not browsing for fun. The interface should feel like talking to someone honest who's on their side — not a sales pitch. They are often on mobile, emotionally invested, and may visit multiple times before deciding.

**Secondary — donors and supporters.** People who can't adopt but want to contribute. They need to understand impact quickly and feel the weight of individual stories, not statistics. They trust the organisation because it doesn't sanitize reality.

**What they're trying to do:** Adopters want to know if *this specific cat* is right for *their specific life*. Donors want to feel that their money matters and lands in a real place.

### Brand Personality

**Defiant. Warm. Honest.**

Oaza says yes when everyone else says no — FIV, FeLV, cancer, road accidents, terminally ill, dying. The brand never pretends this is easy. It doesn't use phrases like "healthy, affectionate" or stock-shelter language. It tells the truth and leans into it. Vulnerability is the superpower.

Voice: direct, unhurried, human. A trusted friend who knows hard things but doesn't catastrophise.
Tone: warm but never saccharine. Defiant without being aggressive. Quietly proud.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Reference: editorial / magazine — Kinfolk, NYT.**

Generous whitespace. Strong, purposeful typography. Photography carries the emotional weight — cats are the subject, not decorations. Layouts breathe. Text is never cramped. Every design decision should ask: *does this give the photo room to speak?*

**Anti-references:** Generic shelter sites (boxy grids, clipart paw prints, Comic Sans energy, busy sidebars). Charity telethon aesthetics. Anything that looks like a form with a database behind it.

**Color palette:**
- `#2D6A4F` (`oaza-green`) — primary brand green, dark forest. Used for hero backgrounds, section headers, emphasis.
- `#F4E8D1` (`oaza-warm`) — warm cream. Background for featured sections. Anchors the warmth.
- `#C1440E` (`oaza-rust`) — burnt rust / terracotta. Primary CTA color, link accents, urgent callouts.
- `stone-*` — neutral grays for body text and subtle borders.

**Visual patterns to keep:**
- Pill / rounded-full buttons always (never rectangular)
- Frosted badge overlays on photos (`bg-white/90 backdrop-blur-sm`)
- Bottom gradient fade on cat photos (`from-black/30 to-transparent`)
- Colored tag chips overlaid bottom-left on photos
- Full-bleed alternating sections: `oaza-warm → white → oaza-green`
- Left green border accent on blockquotes (`w-0.5 bg-oaza-green/30`)
- Large ornamental serif quote mark in `text-oaza-green/40`
- Stats displayed as oversized bold numerals, not tables or charts

**Theme:** Light only. Dark mode is not planned.

**Motion:** Keep animations subtle and purposeful. The marquee carousel uses `paused` on hover. Image zooms use `duration-500` (not fast). No gratuitous effects.

### Design Principles

1. **The cat is the hero.** Photography leads. Copy explains. UI gets out of the way. Never crop a face.

2. **Honesty over comfort.** Show the diagnosis. Name the illness. The audience came here because they want the truth — giving them less is condescending.

3. **Breathe.** Generous padding, whitespace, and line-height. When in doubt, add 4px of margin, not remove it. Dense layouts belong to e-commerce, not stories.

4. **One action per section.** Each section has a single clear CTA. No competing buttons, no option paralysis. Secondary actions are visually quieter (border-only buttons, text links).

5. **Small org, big heart — never look corporate.** No gradients with brand colors on buttons. No shadows on shadows. No rounded corners on rounded corners. Clean, warm, human.
